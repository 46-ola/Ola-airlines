My Journey

Ola Airlines started as a way to share a story I'm still living: the road from a bicycle-shop kind of curiosity to an aeronautical engineering degree — and, eventually, wings that can change shape mid-flight.

Every step that got me closer to aeronautics.

2024 — Present CURRENT

Now in my second year (CP2), specializing in Engineering. Between the Frobenius method, Bessel functions, Markov chains, and ballistic trajectory projects in MATLAB/Octave, this is where the theory starts turning into something I can actually build.

Ahead

The long-term target is a PhD at a top aeronautics research institution — MIT among them — working on adaptive and morphing wing structures.

What I'm Building

Aeronautics isn't just coursework — it's also what I build on my own time.

Personal Project · React

Morphing Wing Simulator

An interactive simulator exploring ailes à géométrie variable (variable-geometry wings) — built around NACA airfoil theory, aerodynamic formulas, and comparisons with real aircraft. It's the project where my coursework and my curiosity about adaptive aerostructures meet.

Field of Interest

Adaptive & Morphing Wing Structures

The research direction I keep coming back to: wings that change shape in flight to optimize lift and drag for different phases of flight, the way a bird's wing does instinctively. Institutions doing serious work here — ISAE-SUPAERO, LARCASE at ÉTS Montréal, TU Delft — are on my radar for where this could eventually lead.
